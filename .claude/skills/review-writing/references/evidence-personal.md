# Review Writing — Evidence Integration: Personal/Application Appendix

## Agent C — Evidence integration: personal evidence standard

In personal essays, "evidence" is specific personal experience, observed detail, and named memory — not citations. Flag claims grounded only in abstraction or generalisation that could be made by anyone.

A claim about one's own experience should be grounded in specificity: not "I began to feel differently" but "I found myself doing X when I had previously done Y." When a claim floats in abstraction, ask: what specific moment or observation is this generalising from? If the answer is not in the text, it should be.

**Flag:** claims about the writer's experience or change that are stated without any specific grounding — where the reader has no access to the actual experience, only the writer's summary of it.

**Retain:** abstractions and generalisations that follow from specific examples already given in the essay. Flag only when abstraction appears without its grounding, not when it concludes from it.

**Citation density adaptation (personal):** Instead of citation density, flag over-reliance on a single anecdote or memory that appears in more than 3 paragraphs without the essay moving forward — the personal equivalent of source over-reliance.
