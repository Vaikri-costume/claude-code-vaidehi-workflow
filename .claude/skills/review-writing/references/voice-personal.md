# Review Writing — Voice Consistency: Personal/Application Appendix

## Agent D — Voice consistency: personal register

Personal essays establish a register (intimate, reflective, essayistic, formal-personal, etc.). Flag:
- Paragraphs where formal academic register intrudes without apparent intentionality into an otherwise intimate or reflective voice
- Paragraphs where the writer adopts a generic "applicant voice" — language that sounds like every other application essay rather than this specific person
- Self-contradiction that appears to be avoided rather than addressed. Authentic personal writing often holds unresolved contradiction; artificial reconciliation of conflicting feelings is a flag. Conversely, unacknowledged self-contradiction may be an oversight — note it neutrally, do not decide which it is.

**Retain:** consistent informal contractions ("I'd", "didn't") if they are consistent with the established register throughout. Flag only inconsistent register, not register you personally dislike.

**Do NOT flag:** genuine tentativeness, hedged personal claims about one's own feelings, or moments of self-doubt — these are authentic markers of personal voice, not failures of confidence.
