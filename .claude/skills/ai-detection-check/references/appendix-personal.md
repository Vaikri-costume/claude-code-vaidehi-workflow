# AI Detection Check — Personal/Application Essay Appendix

This appendix is concatenated after base.md for personal, application, and other non-research essays. It modifies threshold and calibration for patterns 1, 9, and 14.

---

## Pattern 9 — Hedging (DIFFERENT rules for personal writing)

Personal essays use experiential hedging authentically. These are NOT AI patterns in personal writing — do NOT flag them:
- "I think", "I believe", "I felt like", "it seemed to me", "I'm not sure, but", "I wonder if"
- First-person epistemic qualifiers about the writer's own feelings, memories, or perceptions

**Flag only:** hedging that distances the writer from claims they are making about the world, other people, or external events — where the writer is in a position to know and is hedging unnecessarily.

**Retain:** any hedging about internal states, interpretations of personal experience, or acknowledgement of the limits of one's own perspective.

---

## Pattern 1 — Significance inflation (CALIBRATED for personal writing)

Personal essays legitimately make claims about the personal significance of events. Distinguish:

**Retain:** "This experience changed how I thought about X" — personal, bounded, specific claim.
**Flag:** "This experience represents a transformative shift in human understanding" — the scope of significance has inflated beyond the personal.

Flag only when the significance claim extends beyond the writer's own experience to make claims about all people, the human condition, or universal truth without earning them.

---

## Pattern 14 — Announcement sentences (DIFFERENT threshold for personal)

"In this essay I want to explore..." as the opening sentence of a personal essay is sometimes an authentic stylistic choice — it can signal genuine tentativeness or personal investment.

**Do NOT flag:** a single announcement sentence at the opening of a personal essay, if it sounds like it was written with a specific voice rather than generated.
**Flag:** announcement sentences that appear mid-essay, or that appear in every section, or that use the impersonal third-person ("This essay will") rather than first-person ("I want to").

---

## Positive authenticity signals (note but do not flag)

These are markers of authentic personal voice — when you find them, note them as strengths rather than ignoring them:
- Specific sensory details (colour, texture, sound, smell)
- Named people and specific places
- Precise dates or time markers
- Self-correction or uncertainty ("or maybe it was — I can't quite remember")
- Unexpected specificity: the kind of detail that AI tends not to generate because it serves no rhetorical function other than truth
- Unresolved contradictions: when the writer holds two conflicting feelings without forcing reconciliation

These signals do not cancel flags. Report them separately as observed strengths.
