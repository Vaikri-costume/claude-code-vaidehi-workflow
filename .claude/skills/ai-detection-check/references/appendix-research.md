# AI Detection Check — Research Essay Appendix

This appendix is concatenated after base.md for research essays. It modifies threshold and calibration for patterns 5, 7, and 9.

---

## Pattern 9 — Hedging threshold (HIGHER for research)

Research essays in humanities fields hedge interpretive claims by disciplinary convention. Excessive hedging (Pattern 9) has a **higher** flagging threshold for research essays — only flag hedging when it is incommensurate with the claim's actual interpretive uncertainty.

Apply the hedging calibration criteria from base.md strictly:
- Flag: hedged verifiable facts, hedged direct observations, tautological hedging
- Retain: interpretive claims about cultural meaning, historical causation, contested theory, attribution of intention to social structures
- The bar for "this should hedge" is genuinely high in humanities research

When uncertain: report as "hedging — context-dependent" with the specific claim.

---

## Pattern 7 — Vague attributions (LOWER threshold for research)

Vague attributions are **more** problematic in research essays than in personal writing. "Scholars note" without a specific citation is not acceptable in academic writing — it performs authority without providing it.

Flag all instances of vague attribution in research essays. The repair is always specific: name the scholar, provide the citation.

---

## Pattern 5 — AI vocabulary calibration

Some terms in Pattern 5 are field-standard in specific disciplines. If the vocabulary register (from context loading) includes a term as domain-standard, do NOT flag it as AI vocabulary.

Examples: "nuanced" as a precise qualifier in postcolonial studies; "landscape" as a specific term in urban geography or ecology. When the vocabulary register confirms field-standard use: note "retained — field-standard per vocabulary register."

If no vocabulary register was provided: apply standard Pattern 5 criteria and note the absence.
